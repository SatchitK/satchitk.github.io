Research Roles

I am currently a part of Kwan Lab's Bioinformatics Team as an undergraduate researcher. Currently, I am training in a suite of Bioinformatics tools, including various programming languages. Some relevant languages include R, Python, and Bioinformatics workflow tools like Snakemake.

My undergraduate research project for Summer Research Symposium focuses on observing differences between various groups of control mice used in our experiments. I work on analyzing previous data and trying to find correlations between various groups in question. Then, we use plotting tools in R to make volcano plots (see image on the left), which graphs differential gene expression. This is helpful in determining differnces between various groups, and possibly helping us find a conclusion regarding control groups and their role in an experimental scenario.
