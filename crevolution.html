Projects

I joined Crevolution Robotics, a high school FIRST Robotics Team, my sophomore year in 2020. FIRST Robotics is an annual high school robotics competition, where a challenge is announced in January, and teams are allocated 2-3 months to build a fully functioning robot to tackle the challenge. These challenges are often relevant to ongoing world issues, and require lots of innovative solutions.

Crevolution Robotics, like many other teams, was divided into "sub-teams," which included CAD Designing, Programming, Building, Electrical, and other non-technical subteams focused on PR, Marketing, and Business. I was part of the Programming Subteam, where I served as a member during 2020-2022, and as the lead programmer during 2022-2023 season. Some of my major responsibilties and accomplishments included the following:

1. Robot Programming: Robot programming was done using Java programming language, along with several helper libraries written in other languages such as Python. The competition included an Autonomous Robot Driving portion, which led me to utilize path generation software (see image/video attached). Path generation created "waypoints" that the robot drivetrain followed to complete the tasks it was designed to do, such as scoring cones/cubes or shooting basketball sized inflatable balls in a hoop. To further improve the robot's performance, we made use of industry-inspired Computer Vision tools, such as OpenCV. These tools incorporated the Retroreflective and AprilTags targets on the competition field to more accurately target certain fiducial markers (see images). This was also coded using Java Libraries and used OrangePI 5 hardware to run the program, along with a 1080p Camera.

2. Android App Development: For my two seasons on Crevolution, I was involved in Android App Development for collecting data on other teams in the competition, using data analysis to parse thru our results and predict the best partners for winning the competition. As part of this role, I was able to extensively learn the ins and outs of Android Studio, how hardware emulation works, and how to generalize an app to work on many devices. We were able to successfully develop apps for small 7 in. tablets, and even distribute this software to fellow competition teams and help them utilize our software successfully.

3. Leadership and Instruction: One of the main roles of being the software lead on our team was instructing new members regarding Java, Robot Programming, and Android Development. For this, I was able to create a framework of PowerPoint presentations, YouTube Videos, and supplmentary information materials. This helped students understand Java, implement small programs of their own, and test their code on practice Robots. In the 2023 season, I was able to successfully lead a team of 10 members to code the robot from ground up, ensuring it was ready for competition.

4. Mentorship and Community Service: Being part of a FIRST Robotics team, we are enabled to help out in our local community. As part of Crevolution, I was able to mentor elementary students excel in FIRST Lego Robot Competition, which included smaller-scale LEGO Robots competiting in a challenge each year. This allowed me to pass on the knowledge I had gained regarding Robot Development process and inspire the younger students about STEM and its endless opportunities. With the help of Crevolution, we were able to start 3 new teams in the Utica Schools District, and ensure success for those teams, helping them reach State Level competition multiple years in a row.
